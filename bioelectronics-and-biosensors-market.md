<p><p>The bioelectronics and biosensors market includes various types, each employing distinct principles. Electrochemical biosensors analyze biochemical reactions through electrical signals, commonly used for glucose monitoring. Thermal biosensors measure temperature changes during reactions for applications like metabolic studies. Piezoelectric biosensors utilize mechanical vibrations to detect mass changes, ideal for pathogen detection. Optical biosensors leverage light interactions to identify biomolecules, widely applied in medical diagnostics and environmental monitoring. Each type plays a crucial role in advancing healthcare and scientific research.</p></p>
