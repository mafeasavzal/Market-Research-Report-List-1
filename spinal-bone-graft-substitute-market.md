<p><p>The spinal bone graft substitute market comprises four main types: </p><p>1. **Allograft**: Bone harvested from human donors, processed to ensure safety and used to promote healing. </p><p>2. **Synthetic Bone Graft**: Artificial materials tailored to mimic natural bone properties, promoting effective integration. </p><p>3. **Xenograft**: Bone grafts derived from animal sources, commonly porcine, used to support spinal fusion. </p><p>4. **Autograft**: Tissue taken from the patient's own body, typically providing the best biological compatibility and healing outcomes. Each type serves specific needs in spinal surgery.</p></p>
