<p><p>The hormonal contraceptive implant market is primarily divided into two types: Levonorgestrel contraceptive implants and Etonogestrel contraceptive implants. Levonorgestrel implants are progestin-based and are typically effective for up to five years, preventing ovulation and thickening cervical mucus. Etonogestrel implants, also progestin-based, offer similar mechanisms but can last for up to three years. Both types provide long-term, reversible contraception options, appealing to individuals seeking a convenient and low-maintenance birth control solution without the daily or monthly regimen of other methods.</p></p>
