<p><p>Magnesium matrix composites are categorized into cast and wrought types based on their manufacturing processes. Cast-type magnesium matrix composites are produced by pouring molten magnesium and reinforcing materials into molds, allowing for complex shapes and cost-effective production. Wrought-type composites, on the other hand, are created through deformation processes like extrusion or rolling, which enhance their mechanical properties and uniformity. Both types serve various applications, including aerospace, automotive, and electronic industries, driven by their lightweight and high-strength characteristics.</p></p>
