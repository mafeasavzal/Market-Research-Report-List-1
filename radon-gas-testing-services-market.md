<p><ul><li>Passive</li><li>Active</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radon gas testing services encompass two primary market types: passive and active. Passive testing involves placing detectors in a designated area to absorb radon over a specific period, typically measuring levels without external influence. This method is cost-effective and straightforward. In contrast, active testing employs electronic devices to continuously monitor radon levels, providing real-time data and immediate results. Active testing is generally more precise and suitable for real-time assessments, making it ideal for urgent scenarios or detailed evaluations.</p></p>
